Motivation

Housing inequality is a significant issue in the United States, affecting access to safe, affordable, and quality housing for millions of people.

Expectation: Visualizing Housing Inequality in the United States (2016-2020). Our project examines housing inequality in the United States using data from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, spanning the years 2016 to 2020.

Factors contribute to the complexities of housing inequality:

  • Income Disparities: Significant gaps in income levels create unequal opportunities for individuals to access safe, affordable, and quality housing. Rising housing costs often outpace wage growth, placing a strain on low- and middle-income households.
  • Location and Housing Characteristics: Housing markets vary considerably across different locations. Urban areas frequently experience higher housing costs and limited availability, while rural communities may face challenges with aging housing stock and lack of infrastructure.
  • Demographic Factors: Race, ethnicity, age, and family structure can influence an individual's experience in the housing market. Systemic biases and discriminatory practices can lead to disparities in access to housing opportunities and fair lending practices.

Motivating Questions:

  • What are the primary factors contributing to housing inequality across the U.S.?
  • How do geographic and demographic trends impact access to housing?
  • Can data-driven insights inform policy changes to promote equitable housing opportunities?

Goals

  • Uncover Trends and Patterns: Analyze the chosen datasets to identify trends and patterns in housing inequality across different states in the U.S. from 2016 to 2020.
  • Reveal Driving Factors: Investigate the key factors contributing to housing inequality, including income disparities, geographical variations, and demographic influences.
  • Inform Policy and Solutions: Provide insights that could help inform policies and interventions aimed at addressing housing inequality and promoting equitable access to safe and affordable housing for all.
